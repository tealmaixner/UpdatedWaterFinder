About Water Finder

Issue:

Nearly 1 million people in California do not have access to safe drinking water and its estimated that 370,000 Californians rely on tap water that may contain high levels arsenic, nitrate or hexavalent chromium.

Mission Statement

To give nonprofit organizations a platform to local community members and provide clean drinking water in the Los Angeles Area.

Community

Non-profit organizations, water shortage activists, community leaders, volunteers, and future donor, and more can be involved.

Easy Access

Visitors can easily fill out a contact form to find out more information about locations and organizations.

Who we Serve

Any individual lacking access to clean water including: Homeless individuals and Rural Communities.

California is also experiencing one of the worst droughts in decades. With these two issues homeless people are experiencing a lot of challenges accessing safe drinking basic resources like food and water . With the rising water restrictions.

California has one of the largest homeless population in the country. In 2020, the most recent federal data shows that California is experiencing 161,500 individual's with a homeless problem. Other sources suggest the homeless situation has continued to get worse over these last two years by about 22,500 In just two years plus the pandemic.
